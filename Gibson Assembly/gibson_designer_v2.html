Gibson Assembly Designer

validated NN Tm · specificity-screened overlaps

Design PCR primers that make each part Gibson-ready. Overlaps are sized to clear the reaction temperature, and every binding region and overlap arm is screened for off-target homology — against its own source plasmid, every other primer's target region, and every other fragment — so you don't amplify or assemble the wrong thing.

How this tool designs

Expert Gibson practice, built in

📏
≥20 bp overlaps, GC-favoured. 20 bp is the accepted floor at the 50 °C reaction temperature (NEBuilder/SnapGene); the tool never goes below it, and prefers higher-GC arms when several options tie.
🧩
Whole-fragment overlaps first. Where possible the entire overlap is drawn from one flanking fragment's own tail/head — simplest, fewest primers modified — falling back to a split only if that's not unique.
🔎
Both binding region and overlap are homology-screened against every template and fragment, with mismatch tolerance (near-repeats count, not just exact ones) — the Primer-BLAST principle applied to Gibson design.
🌡️
Binding Tm matched F/R, 3′ GC clamp checked. Binding and overlap Tm are tracked separately — only the binding region matters for PCR annealing temperature.
Also worth doing at the bench: gel-purify PCR products before assembly if any non-specific bands appear; keep template plasmid (not PCR product) proportion low if linearizing by PCR; use a 1–2:1 insert:vector molar ratio as a starting point.
